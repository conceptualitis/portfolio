My best customer service experience.

There are a few items that are incredibly hard to buy, primarily for the effort that goes into shopping for them. Mattresses and cars are pretty hard, sure, but it seems that strollers are the most difficult thing to buy. You'll have guilt colliding with a desire for frugality all mingling with wanting to provide the best for your kid. Which is what makes the pleasant experience we had buying ours so exceptional.

When we walked into the store that day our objective was to try out the two brands of stroller we'd discovered during our research. Upon inspection, each of them had glaring practicality issues — one being unstable and the other was far too heavy to lift regularly (it could, however, assemble itself — for real, check it out if you want.)

Routed by this turn of events we considered leaving the store when Holly approached us. She started by having us try out a number of different strollers we hadn't previously considered — we pushed them around, adding/removing attachments to make it as close to the perfect stroller we didn't know we could have. After figuring out the configuration we wanted, Holly sat us down to discuss color schemes, providing suggestions when we got stuck. She recommended color schemes and materials that were the most durable — her expertise with the products was evident. Holly, as it turned out, was a former Beverly Hills nanny which is like having the purple heart of stroller selection. Settling on a sand and black theme, she let us know that the attachments could also be customized. She even threw in the cup holder for free.

If I were to break it down I'd say there were three key components to this being a great experience.

  1. Getting something for free, which establishes trust. I'd expect these things to be items which sweeten the deal but don't account for much of the total sale.
  2. Patience was a huge factor. Without her taking over an hour to walk us through all the various options there was no way we would've found our stroller and certainly no way we'd buy at that store.
  3. How much she knew and how much she was willing to demonstrate. You'll assemble the stroller for us and show us how to collapse it and reconfigure it? Great!
